Home Source Quiz

Official U.S. Government Websites:

  • Congress.gov: This is the official website for U.S. federal legislative information. It provides comprehensive information about bills, resolutions, and legislative activities.

Government and Civic Education Websites:

  • USA.gov: The U.S. government's official web portal provides a wide range of information about the branches of government, including Congress.
  • Civic Education: The Center for Civic Education offers resources and information about U.S. government and civics education, including information about Congress.

Educational and Nonprofit Organizations:

  • The Library of Congress: The Library of Congress provides extensive resources on American history, government, and Congress.
  • The United States Senate: The official website of the U.S. Senate offers information about its history, functions, and members.
  • The United States House of Representatives: The official website of the U.S. House of Representatives provides information about its functions and members.

News and Media Outlets:

News organizations like The New York Times, The Washington Post, and CNN often cover the activities and functions of Congress, including legislative matters.
